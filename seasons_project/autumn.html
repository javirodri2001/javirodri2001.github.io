Autumn

British word to refer to this season probably comes from a Latin word (both Spanish and French languages use a word that sounds relatively close to autumn) that means something profound or perhaps comes from Ancient Roman mitology, which sounds pretty douchey to me. However, it is still better that the US term "fall." We call it here fall, because the leaves "fall" from the trees. (read as if you were Ralph Wiggun)
